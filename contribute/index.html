We <3 the people who are involved in this project, and we’d love to have you on board. And here’s how you can support us:

Share your skills and knowledge:

Hoodie is still work in progress, and you can help us make it more awesome. Support us in working towards Hoodie’s eventual goals and making Hoodie a tool which enables people to express themselves through an well-accessible, easy-to-learn technology. No matter your level of skills or knowledge, we appreciate your support and would love to welcome you on board.

Frontend Development

We need you, frontend development people! Build your own app based on Hoodie or help us with our currently open frontend development issues. If you have any questions, don’t hesitate to get in touch!

Backend Development

In order to enable many people so they can finally build apps without having to think about backends, Hoodie of course needs a backend itself. If you want to help with backend development, take a look at these currently open issues or contact us!


Extend Hoodie’s core functionalities

Hoodie’s main features like user signup and administration, data storage and shares are all Node.js-based plugins which are added to its core code. We encourage you to write your own plugins and publish them on npm so everyone can use them. In this tutorial you’ll see how building plugins works. If you need any help, let us know.

Write content

We want to provide good content that is useful for people and that helps them understand what Hoodie does, is and stands for. This is why we have a Blog which we regularly add new pieces to, and we have our ”TGIF”, the reading recommendations we curate and publish every week. If you want to support our content team, contact us, we’d love to have you on board!


Write documentation

We’re convinced that a good project is nothing without a good documentation. Documentation is even more important for us who want to empower a wide range of people. If you want to help us by writing documentation yourself, proofreading and testing existing docs, translating content or creating screencasts: there you go!


Give feedback about your experiences with Hoodie and its community

Did you try Hoodie, build something based on it or did you have a nice encounter with the Hoodie community? We want to improve in everything we do, and thus would love to hear your feedback. And here’s a tiny form for that.



Give talks

Did you like Hoodie and now want to talk about it to other people? Wow, that’s amazing to hear! We know that preparing talks takes a lot of time. To support you and lower your efforts, we have prepared some talks about Hoodie for you, including presenter notes. Just click on the format of your choice, download them, adjust them to your needs and help us spread the word! Our currently available talks are:

  • One million Cheesecakes: Getting started with Hoodie: Keynote | PowerPoint
  • Getting started with Hoodie and Offline First: Keynote | PowerPoint

Start a Meetup in your town

Hoodie is both easy-to-learn for beginners and a great tool for experienced developers. So why not start a Hoodie-meetup in your town? Many of us have been organising meetups for a long time now and found these three pieces really helpful:

  • How to organise your own meetup by Kate Kendall
  • A practical guide to organising a successful Meetup by Jordan Lewis
  • 25 Best Practices for Meetup Organizers by Mike Schinkel

If you want to give a Hoodie-talk at your meetup, check the “give talks”-section above, we have talks ready for you to adjust and use. In case you need further support, ping us, we’re happy to help!


Wear Hoodie

Cozy, warm and snuggly: this is Hoodie. And this is what Hoodie textiles are like. You’ll find them all in our Hoodie-Merchandise-Shop. We have some default items there, and some special offers which are only available for a limited time. By getting your own Hoodie-Shirt, Hoodie-Tote or whatever you may like, you also support us financially.


Give money

Hoodie matters to us and we want it to have a good future and be a sustainable project. We also want to rethink the ways Open Source is built and funded, and we want to work towards a cultural change. Thus, we’re trying out different ways to fund it. And you can support us:
